A brief History

Frances E. Allen (1923-2020)




Born in Auust 4,1932 in Peru, New York, U.S and died August 4, 2020. She was a well known computer scientist since she won the A.M Turning Award(it equivalent to the Noble Prize) in 2006 she was the first woman to win the award. Her high school math teacher inpired her to like math, so she decided to become one herself. She went to the New York State College for Teachers(now is State University of New York at Albany) for 4 years and earned her BA in mathematic with a minor in physics. She then went back to the high school she attended and taught algebra and advanced trigonometry for 2 years.

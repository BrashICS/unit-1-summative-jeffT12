A brief History

Frances E. Allen (1923-2020)




Early life and education

Born in August 4,1932 in Peru, New York, U.S and died August 4, 2020. She was the oldest of 6 children. She grew up in a farm with her sibling and parents. Her father was a farmer and her mother was a elementry school teacher. She attended a local High School in her town and graduated from there with the intention of pursuing mathematic. Her math theacher is infact the person who inspired her to keep going with math. She then decided to be a math teacher hersel so, she went to New York State College for teachers(now is call State University of New York) for 4 years where she got her BA in mathematic with a minor in phisycs. After all the time spend in school, she realize that she'll need a master degree in oder to obtain full teaching certification. She went to Columbia University before transferring to the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, where she graduated with a MA in mathematic. She registered in some of the first computing courses ever offered there and learn how to program an IMB 650(Magnetic Drum Data-Processing Machine) and early digital computer made by IMB(International Business Machines) in the mid 1950s. It was the first mass-produced computer in the world. She learn how to program it from Bernard Galler who was co-developer of the MAD programming language and later became president of the ACM and ACM fellow. She got offer a job by IMB because they often hosted meeting at the University of Michigan.


Career and research

She had only the intention of paying off her student debts and then returning to teaching. Instead she stay at IBM for the next 45 years. When she joined IBM in 1957 at New York Poughkeepsie, as a programmer she taught incomming employees the basics of Fortran(third generation compiled imperative programming language that is espa




Award and Honors

She was a well known computer scientist since she won the A.M Turning Award(it equivalent to the Noble Prize) in 2006 she was the first woman to win the award.
